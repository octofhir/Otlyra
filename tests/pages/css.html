What works today

The cascade: the user-agent stylesheet, author rules from <style> and a style= attribute, resolved by origin, specificity and source order, with !important, inheritance, calc(), custom properties and media queries.

Boxes: colours, fonts, margins — including auto margins, which is how a box is centred — padding, borders per side, widths and heights, and text-align. On an inline box too: its padding and borders take room in the line, and a box broken over two lines keeps the border on the edge each piece owns.

Position: relative moves a box without moving its neighbours, absolute takes it out of the flow and places it against the nearest positioned ancestor, and fixed places it against the viewport and leaves it there while the page scrolls.

Flex: display: flex with a direction, a gap, flex-grow and flex-shrink, justify-content, align-items and align-self, and wrapping onto as many lines as the items need. An item with no width of its own is as wide as its content wants to be.

Floats: float and clear, with the lines beside a float shortened one at a time. Stylesheets from <link> are fetched, and so are the pictures an <img> asks for.

Selector matching, over our own DOM: type, class, id, attribute (including ^=, $=, *=), the four combinators, structural pseudo-classes, and :is(), :where() and :not(). The --dump-selectors flag answers "which elements does this selector match" against a real document.

What does not work yet

A rule the cascade computes correctly can still change nothing, because layout reads a subset of what CSS defines: grid has no formatting context behind it, an unsupported display lays out as a block, position: sticky lays out as static, and z-index is not read — a positioned box paints in document order like everything else. In a flex container, order, align-content and baseline alignment are not read, an item may shrink below its content, and inline-flex is placed as a block. A float with no width of its own takes the whole column rather than shrinking to its contents, and only a float establishes a formatting context of its own, so overflow does not yet stop one.

An inline box is as tall as the line it sits on rather than as tall as its own text, and vertical-align does not exist. Border styles other than solid are drawn solid. A resize that no rule reads costs nothing — the sheets are parsed once and the cascade is skipped — but a resize that does change something recomputes every element rather than the part that changed.
