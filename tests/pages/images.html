Images

Every picture on this page is the same three files, drawn at whatever size the rules ask for. The three are 320×240, 200×200 as a JPEG, and 64×64.

Intrinsic size

With nothing said about its size, a picture is drawn at the size it is: — 64 pixels square, sitting in this line of text.

One dimension given

The other comes from the picture's own ratio, so nothing is squashed.

Both given

Then the ratio is not kept, because the page asked for something else.

Attributes rather than CSS

A width and height attribute stand in for the picture's own size, so a rule can still override them: is a 200-pixel JPEG asked for at 48.

Filling the column

width: 100% against the column this page is held to.

In a line, and around one

Pictures sit on the baseline like text does: small, larger, larger again — and the line grows to hold the tallest of them.

A framed one: the border and padding are the inline box model's, not the picture's.

Block-level

A picture with display: block is on a line of its own.

Several at once

Six requests, fetched by a pool rather than one after another.

What is missing

A picture that does not load shows its alt text instead: — and one with no alt shows nothing at all: .

Not implemented, and visible here: srcset and sizes are ignored, so the same file is used at every size; object-fit is not read, so a picture given both dimensions is stretched rather than fitted; there is no vertical-align, so a picture always sits on the baseline; animated formats show their first frame; and nothing is decoded lazily — every picture on the page is fetched before the page is finished.
