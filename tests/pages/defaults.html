Default HTML rendering

Nothing on this page is styled. Everything you see is the user-agent stylesheet — the browser's own opinion about what HTML looks like before any author has said anything. The sections follow the HTML standard's rendering chapter, and where we do not implement something it says so on the page rather than leaving you to notice.

3. Inline semantics

b, strong, i, em, u, s, del, ins, small, mark, code, kbd, samp, var, cite, dfn, abbr, q, time, data, H2O, x2, a link.

Known gaps here: <q> should generate quotation marks, and <sub>/<sup> should be lowered and raised — both need generated content and vertical-align, which are not implemented. They currently differ only in size.

6. Form controls

A checked checkbox, and an unchecked one.

A chosen radio, and one not chosen.

A dropdown:

A text area:

These are text on a tinted background, not native widgets: there is no border, no focus ring, no pressed state, and nothing responds to being clicked. A field's contents come from its value or placeholder attribute, the way a real browser generates them too — it just puts them inside a widget we do not have. A <select> shows the selected option and hides the rest.

Not implemented: table layout. Cells stack as blocks instead of forming rows and columns, and there are no borders or cell spacing. This is what that looks like, and it is not a claim that tables work.
